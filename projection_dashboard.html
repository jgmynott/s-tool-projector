How to read this projection The uncertainty bands are the value here, not the median. Backtested across 5,040 forecasts (15 symbols, 7 years), the 80% band reliably contains ~80% of realized prices — calibration is excellent. However, the median forecast is statistically indistinguishable from "price stays flat" on a 1-year horizon, which is consistent with the random-walk nature of single-stock returns. Use the bands to size your conviction and understand the range of outcomes; do not treat the median as a price target. Real predictive signal requires data this model doesn't yet have (analyst estimates, options-implied vol, macro regime).

Candles (past year)— Daily OHLC for the prior 252 trading days. Green = close above open, red = close below open.
Median (50th)— Most likely projected path. Half of simulations end above this line, half below.
75th — Upside— Optimistic case: only 25% of simulated paths exceed this price.
25th — Downside— Pessimistic case: 75% of simulated paths stay above this price.
90th — Bull case— Strong upside scenario: only 10% of simulations reach this high.
10th — Bear case— Tail-risk downside: 90% of simulations stay above this level.

Glossary

Annualized Drift (mu):The average expected yearly return based on historical log returns — a positive drift means the stock has historically trended upward.
Annualized Volatility (sigma):How much the price swings per year — higher values mean wider projection bands and more uncertainty.
Starting Price (S0):The most recent closing price used as the launch point for all simulated paths.
Daily Drift:The risk-adjusted expected price change per trading day, accounting for volatility drag.
Daily Volatility:The standard deviation of daily returns — drives the randomness in each simulated step.
History Used:Total number of trading days of historical data fed into the model.
SMA Period:The moving average window (200-day or 50-day) used as the equilibrium anchor for mean reversion.
Current MA:Today's moving average value — the price the mean-reversion model expects the stock to gravitate toward.
Trend Slope (ann.):The annualized rate at which the moving average itself is rising or falling — captures the long-term direction.
Kappa (daily):Mean-reversion speed — how quickly the price is pulled back toward the moving average each day. Higher = stronger pull.
Half-Life:Number of trading days for a deviation from the moving average to shrink by half — shorter means faster reversion.
Residual Volatility (ann.):The volatility of price movements that the mean-reversion trend cannot explain — the "noise" around fair value.
20d Momentum:Recent short-term price velocity — applied as a decaying boost to the first ~20 days of the projection.
