How s-tool builds honest projection bands

A transparent look at the Monte Carlo + mean-reversion engine, what signals we tested, and why most of them got killed.

1. Learn the stock's own dynamics

For each ticker, we pull ~2 years of daily closing prices from yfinance and estimate two core parameters:

  • Annualized drift (μ) — the stock's average return tendency, estimated from log-returns.
  • Annualized volatility (σ) — how wildly the stock swings. This is the single most important input — it determines how wide the projection bands are.

We also fit a mean-reversion anchor by regressing the stock's price on its 1-year moving average. If the stock is far above its long-run trend, the engine pulls projections slightly downward; if below, slightly upward. The strength of this pull is controlled by κ (the reversion speed).

Geometric Brownian Motion with mean reversion dS = κ(θ − S)dt + σS·dW

Where θ is the mean-reversion target (from fundamentals + moving-average regression), κ controls how fast the stock reverts, and σ is historical volatility.

2. Simulate thousands of forward paths

The engine runs 10,000 Monte Carlo paths at the configured horizon (default: 252 trading days = ~1 year). Each path is a random walk under the stock's own volatility, pulled by the mean-reversion force.

At the end, we extract percentiles across all 10,000 terminal prices:

  • P10 / P90 — the wide band. 80% of simulated outcomes fall between these.
  • P25 / P75 — the narrower band. 50% of outcomes.
  • P50 — the median outcome. Not a "target price" — it's where the engine expects the stock to be under its own drift + vol + reversion dynamics, nothing more.

This blend of Monte Carlo (for path diversity) and mean reversion (for fundamental anchoring) is what gives the projections their shape. Pure MC alone would just extrapolate drift; pure MR alone would snap everything to a moving average. The blend captures both randomness and the stock's long-run gravity.

3. Validate with MAPE

Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE) is how we measure whether the engine is honest. For every ticker with at least 2 years of history, we run a walk-forward backtest:

  1. Pick a historical date
  2. Run the projection as of that date (using only data available at the time)
  3. Compare the P50 prediction to what actually happened
  4. Repeat across 466 rolling windows from 2018–2025

The resulting MAPE tells you: "On average, the engine's median projection was X% off from reality for this ticker." A MAPE of 15% on a stock with 40% annualized vol is honest. A MAPE of 2% would be a lie.

Every projection on s-tool shows the model's historical MAPE for that ticker. If the MAPE is high, the bands are wide for a reason — the stock is genuinely hard to predict. We don't hide that.

4. What we tested — and killed

Between January and April 2026, we ran comprehensive backtests on every tilt/signal we could source for free or cheap. The test: does adding this signal to the base MC+MR engine improve MAPE by at least 1 percentage point on the full Russell 3000 across 2018–2025?

Signals that require actual capital commitment or regulatory disclosure (not text) may still have alpha — that's our active research direction.

5. What's next

The engine currently ships tilt-free projections — pure MC + MR on the stock's own dynamics. That's the honest baseline. But we're actively researching the next generation of signals:

  • Cross-sectional momentum — the most robust anomaly in academic finance. Relative outperformers tend to continue. Different statistical structure from the macro signals we tested.
  • Factor investing — value, quality, low-volatility. We have the fundamentals data via FMP; backtesting infrastructure is built.
  • Portfolio-level intelligence — instead of projecting one ticker at a time, scanning the full Russell 3000 to surface risk-tiered picks (conservative / moderate / aggressive).

Any signal that makes it into the engine will have passed the same bar: ≥1pp MAPE lift on ≥2,000 symbols across 2018–2025 in walk-forward testing. We'd rather ship nothing than ship noise.

6. Data sources

  • Prices: yfinance (5-year daily OHLCV, cached and refreshed daily)
  • Fundamentals: Financial Modeling Prep (FMP) — PE, debt/equity, ROE, sector, market cap
  • Universe: iShares IWV (Russell 3000 ETF) holdings, refreshed every 24h → 2,609 tickers
  • Macro reference: FRED (VIX, SKEW, risk-free rate for Sharpe calculations)
