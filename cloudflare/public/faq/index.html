FAQ

Questions worth answering.

The things people actually ask before and after they sign up. If something's missing, email hello@s-tool.io.

Is this investment advice?

No. Projections are statistical outputs, not recommendations. Every ticker shows its projection range and the model's historical error on that symbol so you can judge the bands yourself.

Where does the data come from?

Standard US-equity price history, point-in-time regulatory filings, macroeconomic reference series, and institutional-positioning data from public sources. The Russell 3000 universe is refreshed daily.

Why kill the sentiment signal?

Because it failed. Rigorous out-of-sample testing across thousands of symbols and hundreds of rolling windows showed negligible edge post-2023, with worse hit rates. We don't ship dead tilts — even if they once were useful. See the "How" page for the full signal graveyard.

What's the difference between Pro and Strategist?

Pro ($8/mo) is for running projections on the full Russell 3000 yourself — 10 a day, with PDF export. Strategist ($29/mo) is the portfolio layer: we rank the universe nightly, attach SEC filings to each pick, and track realized returns. Pro = the tool. Strategist = the intelligence.

Can I cancel anytime?

Yes. Stripe, month-to-month, no contract. Cancel from your account portal — access continues through the end of the billing period, then reverts to Free.

How is my data handled?

Email + Stripe customer ID are stored in a private database, never committed to source. The projection cache is open and shared (no PII). We don't sell or share user data, and we don't train models on what you click.

How often do picks refresh?

Nightly. The daily scan runs at 11:00 UTC (after US post-close refresh), projects every ticker in the universe, applies QC filters (no negative-ER picks, no noise-adjacent Sharpe), and publishes. Historical picks are logged to a ledger so realized returns can be computed on each.

What's the track record?

Live on the Picks page. Returns are computed from each pick's entry price (day it was first published) versus the latest cached price. Only picks with at least 7 days of aging count toward the stats.
